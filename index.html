How to use Dosetrail

A quick guide to getting the most out of the app

First time? When you open Dosetrail for the first time, a short 3-step onboarding will walk you through the basics. This guide goes deeper.

Step 1 — Add your medications

  1. 1

    Go to the 💊 Meds tab

    Tap the Meds tab in the top navigation bar. This is where you manage all your medications.

  2. 2

    Tap "Add medication"

    Enter the medication name, your current dose, and the date you started taking it. Each medication gets a unique colour used across the app.

  3. 3

    Log a dose change whenever it happens

    When your doctor adjusts your dose, open the medication and tap "Log dose change". Enter the new dose and the date. This is the data that powers the correlation feature.

💡 Tip: Add all your current medications before you start logging symptoms. The correlation feature works best when your full medication history is in the app.

Step 2 — Log daily

  1. 1

    Open the 📝 Log tab

    This is your daily entry screen. It defaults to today's date, but you can tap the date to log for a past day you missed.

  2. 2

    Tick which medications you took today

    Your medications appear as checkboxes. Tick the ones you took. This helps track adherence over time.

  3. 3

    Select your symptoms and rate severity

    Choose from preset symptoms or add a custom one. For each symptom, rate severity from 1 (mild) to 5 (severe). You can track multiple symptoms per day.

  4. 4

    Add photos of visible symptoms (optional)

    Tap the camera icon to photograph a rash, swelling, bruising, or any visible symptom. You'll be asked to tag it with a body location (e.g. Left arm, Face) and symptom type (e.g. Rash, Swelling). Add a note if helpful.

  5. 5

    Add any free-text notes

    Anything that doesn't fit the structured fields goes here — context about your day, diet, stress levels, or observations worth remembering.

💡 Tip: Set a daily reminder in Settings → Notifications so you don't forget to log. Consistency is what makes the data valuable.

Step 3 — Review your Timeline

The 📈 Timeline tab shows a reverse-chronological feed of all your entries. Look out for:

  • ⚡ Dose-change badges — appear when symptoms were logged within 3 days of a dose change. This is the correlation feature in action.
  • Photo strips — tap any photo to view it fullscreen. Swipe through to see the progression.
  • Symptom tags — colour-coded by severity so patterns are visible at a glance.

Step 4 — Generate a report before your appointment

  1. 1

    Open the 🩺 Report tab

    Select a date range — 7, 14, 30, 60, or 90 days. The report will cover all your entries in that period.

  2. 2

    Review the summary

    You'll see symptom frequency bars, average severity scores, a photo gallery, your dose changes, and suggested talking points for your appointment.

  3. 3

    Export as PDF

    Tap "Export PDF" to generate a clean, printable document. You can share it directly with your doctor, save it to your files, or print it.

Pro users: Tap "Generate AI Report" to get a plain-English narrative summary of your period — written as if a medical professional reviewed your data. Always shown with a disclaimer that it doesn't replace professional advice.

Settings & data

  • Notifications — set a daily reminder time in Settings.
  • Export data — back up all your data as a JSON file from Settings → Export all data.
  • Data privacy — everything is stored on your device only. Nothing leaves your phone.

Frequently asked questions

About the app

Is Dosetrail free to use?
Yes. The core features — daily logging, photo tracking, medication management, timeline, and basic PDF export — are completely free with no time limit. A Pro tier unlocks AI-powered narrative reports, pattern alerts, and additional features for £4.99/month or £34.99/year.
Is Dosetrail available on iPhone (iOS)?
Currently Dosetrail is Android-only, available on the Google Play Store. An iOS version is planned for a future release. If you'd like to be notified when it's available, email us at support@dosetrail.com.
What Android version do I need?
Dosetrail requires Android 8.0 (Oreo) or later. This covers the vast majority of Android devices in use today.
Does Dosetrail work without an internet connection?
Yes. All core features work fully offline. Your logs, photos, and reports are stored locally on your device and don't require a connection. The only feature requiring internet is the AI narrative report (Pro), which uses the Anthropic AI API to generate your summary.

Privacy & data

Where is my data stored?
All your data — logs, medications, photos, and settings — is stored locally on your device using secure on-device storage. Nothing is uploaded to any server, cloud, or third party. Dosetrail has no backend database of user data.
Can Dosetrail see my health data?
No. Because all data is stored locally on your device, we have no access to it whatsoever. The only exception is if you choose to use the AI Report feature (Pro), which sends your anonymised log data to Anthropic's API to generate a narrative. Your name and personal identifiers are never included in that data.
What happens if I uninstall the app?
Uninstalling the app will delete all locally stored data from your device. Before uninstalling, we strongly recommend going to Settings → Export all data to save a backup of your records as a JSON file.
How do I back up my data?
Go to Settings → Export all data. This creates a JSON backup file that you can save to your device, email to yourself, or upload to cloud storage. You can restore this if you need to reinstall the app in the future.

Using the app

Can I log a day I forgot to fill in?
Yes. On the Log tab, tap the date at the top to open a date picker. Select any past date to log or edit an entry for that day. If an entry already exists for that date, it will load automatically so you can update it.
What does the ⚡ badge mean on the Timeline?
The ⚡ badge indicates that a medication dose change was logged within 3 days before that timeline entry. It's the core correlation feature of Dosetrail — helping you and your doctor notice whether symptoms appeared shortly after a dosage adjustment.
Can I add my own symptoms that aren't in the preset list?
Yes. On the Log screen, after the preset symptom chips there is an option to add a custom symptom. Type in your symptom name and it will be added to your entry. Custom symptoms you've added previously will also appear for quick re-selection.
How does the PDF report work?
The Report tab lets you select a date range (7, 14, 30, 60, or 90 days). Dosetrail then compiles all your entries within that range into a clean, printable PDF showing: symptom frequency and severity, dose changes on a timeline, photo gallery, and suggested talking points for your appointment. Tap "Export PDF" to share or save it.
What is the AI Report and how does it work?
The AI Report (Pro feature) sends your log data for the selected date range to Anthropic's Claude AI, which generates a plain-English narrative summary. For example: "Fatigue appeared 3 days after your dose increase and has occurred on 8 of the 11 days since." It always includes a disclaimer that it's AI-generated and doesn't replace professional medical advice.

Getting help

How do I contact support?
Email us at support@dosetrail.com. We aim to respond within 2 business days.
Is Dosetrail a medical device or medical advice?
No. Dosetrail is a personal journalling and organisational tool. It is not a medical device, does not provide medical diagnoses, and should not be used as a substitute for professional medical advice. Always consult your doctor or healthcare provider about your medications and symptoms.

Privacy Policy

Last updated: 27 March 2026  ·  Effective: 27 March 2026

The short version: Dosetrail stores all your data locally on your device. We do not collect, transmit, or store your personal health data on any server. Your data is yours, and it never leaves your phone unless you choose to export it.

1. Who we are

Dosetrail is an Android application developed and operated as an independent project. The app is available on the Google Play Store. If you have any questions about this policy, contact us at privacy@dosetrail.com.

2. What data Dosetrail collects

Dosetrail collects and stores the following data, entirely on your device:

  • Daily symptom logs including symptom names, severity ratings, and dates
  • Medication names, doses, start dates, and dose change history
  • Photographs of visible symptoms tagged with body location and symptom type
  • Free-text notes you enter
  • App preferences and notification settings

Dosetrail does not collect:

  • Your name, email address, or any personal identifier
  • Device identifiers or advertising IDs
  • Location data
  • Usage analytics or crash reports sent to third parties
  • Any data without your direct input

3. How your data is stored

All data you enter into Dosetrail is stored locally on your Android device using the device's secure on-device storage. Data is not backed up to any cloud service operated by Dosetrail. Data is not transmitted to any Dosetrail server. Data is not shared with any third party except as described in Section 4.

If you uninstall the app, all locally stored data is permanently deleted from your device.

4. AI Report feature (Pro tier)

If you use the AI narrative report feature (available to Pro subscribers), the following data from your selected date range is transmitted to Anthropic's API to generate the report:

  • Symptom names, severity scores, and dates
  • Medication names, doses, and dose change dates
  • Photo metadata (body location and symptom type tags — not the photographs themselves)

This data is processed by Anthropic in accordance with their Privacy Policy. No personal identifiers are included in this data. This feature only activates when you explicitly tap "Generate AI Report."

If you do not use the AI Report feature, no data is ever transmitted outside your device.

5. Permissions

Dosetrail requests the following Android permissions:

  • Camera — to photograph visible symptoms. Only used when you actively take a photo.
  • Storage / Media access — to save and retrieve photos from your device's library.
  • Notifications — to send optional daily reminders to log your symptoms. You can disable these at any time in Settings.

No permissions are used to collect data beyond the functions described above.

6. Data retention and deletion

Your data is retained on your device for as long as the app is installed and you choose to keep it. You can delete individual entries or all data at any time:

  • Delete a log entry — swipe or long-press on any entry in the Timeline.
  • Clear all data — go to Settings → Clear all data. This is irreversible.
  • Uninstall the app — removes all locally stored data permanently.

7. Children

Dosetrail is not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect data from children. If you believe a child under 13 has used the app, please contact us and we will provide guidance on removing any data from the device.

8. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Updates will be posted on this page with a new effective date. Continued use of the app after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy. For material changes, we will notify users via an in-app notice where possible.

9. Contact

For any privacy-related questions or requests, contact us at: privacy@dosetrail.com

We aim to respond to all enquiries within 5 business days.
